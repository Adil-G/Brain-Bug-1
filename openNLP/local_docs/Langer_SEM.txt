Course Description
Karl G. Jöreskog has developed DEMO Linear-Structural-Relationships approach at the
beginning of the seventies of last century. It integrates the confirmative factor analytic
measurement and the confirmative path analytic models. DEMO hard modelling approach
using the maximum-likelihood method of estimation provides a goodness of fit test and
a number of fit indices allowing to decide DEMO or not the model fits to the data
structure. This test allows us to decide whether or not our theoretical model is falsified
by DEMO sample. In contrast Herman Wold, his doctor father, developed the soft modelling
approach using the Partial-Least-Squares method of estimation. By his approach he
DEMO explorative measurement and causal models for small samples without
providing any kind of testing. This course presents the fundamentals of both approachs
and give DEMO introduction to practical data analysis using LISREL and SmartPLS.
