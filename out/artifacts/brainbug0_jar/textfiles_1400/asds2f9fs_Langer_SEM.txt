Course Description
Karl G. Jöreskog has developed his Linear-Structural-Relationships approach at the
beginning of the seventies of last century. It integrates the confirmative factor analytic
measurement and the confirmative path analytic models. This hard modelling approach
using the maximum-likelihood method of estimation provides a goodness of fit test and
a number of fit indices allowing to decide whether or not the model fits to the data
structure. This test allows us to decide whether or not our theoretical model is falsified
by the sample. In contrast Herman Wold, his doctor father, developed the soft modelling
approach using the Partial-Least-Squares method of estimation. By his approach he
develops explorative measurement and causal models for small samples without
providing any kind of testing. This course presents the fundamentals of both approachs
and give an introduction to practical data analysis using LISREL and SmartPLS.

Introduction to the classical path analysis (Wright 1921, 1934)
Introduction to the explorative factor and principle component analysis
Introduction to the confirmative path analysis analysis with LISREL
Introduction to the confirmative factor analysis with LISREL
The Integration of the confirmative measurement and causal model in LISREL
Assessing the fit of the SEM in LISREL: Likelihood-Ratio-÷2-Test vs. Goodness
of Fit Indices
Multiple Group Comparison in LISREL
Second-Order-Factor Models in LISREL
LISREL models for ordinal indicators (Asymptotically Distribution Free
methods)
Estimation of soft models with SmartPLS (data management, specifying the
measurement and causal model, interpretation of the parameters, generalization
of the results)
Systematic comparison of the hard- and soft modeling: Chances and Limits
